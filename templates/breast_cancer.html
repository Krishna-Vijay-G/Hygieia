About These Measurements

These cytological features are computed from digitized images of fine needle aspirate (FNA) of breast masses. They describe characteristics of the cell nuclei present in the image.

Size Features:
  • Radius - size of nuclei
  • Perimeter - boundary length
  • Area - total nuclear area
Shape Features:
  • Compactness - shape regularity
  • Concavity - indentation severity
  • Symmetry - nuclear symmetry
Texture Features:
  • Texture - surface variation
  • Smoothness - radius variation
  • Fractal dimension - complexity
Analysis typically takes 2-3 seconds to complete
Professional Consultation Required

These cytological measurements are typically obtained through medical procedures such as fine needle aspiration (FNA) performed by qualified healthcare professionals.

  • Pathologist: For cytological analysis and measurement interpretation
  • Oncologist: For comprehensive breast cancer evaluation
  • Radiologist: For imaging studies and guided biopsies
  • Surgeon: For tissue sampling and treatment planning
